Trends & Performance Dashboard

AVG Daily Rate By Neighbourhood

The data suggests that the average daily rates of listings by location has a significatn correlation with the neighbourhood and property type.

AVG Occupancy Rate By Listing Type

The data suggests that a slight preference towards Private Room or Entire Home listings over Hotel Rooms and Shared Rooms. This results in these types of listings being vacant for less amount of time through the year.

Seasonal Trends

The data suggests that Autumn is the most active time of the year that listings are occupied. An productive action could be decreasing the average daily rate during the Winter and Spring months to increase total occupancy throughout the year resulting in greater profits.

Host and Property Insights

# of Reviews & AVG Rating Correlation

The data suggests that an increase in reviews has little to no correlation to the average rating a consumer will reward a specific Airbnb listing. Which for the region of Brisbane, QLD is between 4.0 and 4.2 when excluding outliers like listings with less that 5 reviews.

AVG Price in Each Neighbourhood

The data suggests that there is virtually no change in Airbnb prices over the past year and that the average price for an airbnb listing ranges between 50 and 200. There does seem to be an outlier in Pullenvale that could be the result of a Host changing, remvoing or adding a listing. Data going back further in years would be beneficial to show a clearer picture of how pricing has changed over time in Brisbane.

AVG Price By Room Type

The data suggests that Hotel Room's have the highest average cost at $246 per night and Private Room listings have the lowest average cost at $124. This data shows a similar trend in the prices not changing over time because the data set is from 6/7/2024 to 6/6/2025.
